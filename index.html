The Four H's

The decline of wild salmon populations in the Pacific Northwest have been attributed to a variety of factors, denoted the title, The Four H's: hydro, habitat, harvest, and hatcheries. Hydro referring to hydroelectrical dams that impede salmon run, habitat degredation from human interference, over-fishing/over-harvesting, and supplemental salmon produced by hatcheries that compete for recourses with the wild salmon.
