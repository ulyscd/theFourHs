Hydro

Hydroelectrical dams that are used to produce renewable energy have been shown to impede the migration of salmon. The dams block the passage of salmon, preventing them from reaching their spawning grounds. The dams also change the flow of the river, which can affect the water temperature as well as the amount of oxygen in the water. These changes can have a negative impact on the salmon population, leading to a decline in their numbers. A promising solution for the betterment of wild stock involves selective removal of key dams.
