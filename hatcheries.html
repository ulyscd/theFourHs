Hatcheries

Hatcheries produce millions of salmon each year to supplement wild populations. However, these hatchery fish can have negative impacts on wild populations by competing for resources and interbreeding with wild fish. These interbred and supplemental fish lack the natural gene to return to their native streams and spawn. Hatcheries are essential to the continuation of fishing in its current abundance and the traditions of both native American communities as well as commercial and non-commercial fisherman. Proposing a potential reduction in hatchery production may allow an increase in wild population, but it could undermine the economic and cultural importance of salmon to the region.
